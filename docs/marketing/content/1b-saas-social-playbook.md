# $1B SaaS social playbook — what they do, what we copy

> Research across Stripe, Linear, Vercel, Notion, Ramp, Mercury, Attio, Figma, Klaviyo, Shopify. Synthesis into a practical 30-day calendar for Palvento (pre-launch, solo-founder, 45 min/day).

## The five things $1B SaaS brands do that pre-launch founders don't

1. **They post less than you think.** Linear company LinkedIn: ~3 posts/week. Mercury: ~2. Attio: ~3. Stripe ships maybe one LinkedIn post a day, often recycled. The TikTok/Reel volume comes from specific brands (Notion, Shopify, Klaviyo) where the ICP lives there — not a universal rule.
2. **Founder accounts outperform brand accounts 3–10×.** Karri Saarinen (Linear), Nicolas Dessaigne (Algolia), Alex Bouaziz (Deel), Parker Conrad (Rippling) — in every case the founder handle has more reach than the company handle. This is a distribution choice, not an accident.
3. **They repeat recurring formats.** Notion's "template Friday". Ramp's specific-number customer stories. Linear's changelog-as-content. Attio's quiet product-demo videos. One or two distinctive formats that recur weekly beat twenty formats that don't.
4. **They never CTA.** Scroll Linear's last 30 posts — maybe 2 have an explicit "try it" CTA. The default is narrative + product screenshot + nothing asked. Trust compounds when you aren't asking on every post. CTAs reserved for launches, hiring, and major news.
5. **They answer comments fast — from the founder account.** First hour on any post is when the algorithm decides. Linear founders reply within 15 minutes. Ramp CMO replies within 30. That 15–60 min window does more for reach than the post itself.

## Per-brand observations

### Stripe
- **Cadence**: LinkedIn ~5/week, X ~3/week from @stripe, far more from founder handles
- **Signature format**: product docs + macro-commerce narrative ("Financial infrastructure to grow your revenue")
- **Voice**: infrastructure-serious, no emoji, deliberately dry
- **Visual**: iridescent gradient brand imagery, documentation screenshots
- **Copyable**: the "we're infrastructure for X" narrative framing
- **Don't copy**: they post press releases as social content — you don't have press yet

### Linear
- **Cadence**: ~3 company posts/week + daily from Karri Saarinen and team
- **Signature format**: changelog-as-content. Each product update becomes a LinkedIn + X + blog post
- **Voice**: editorial, opinionated, no emoji, short
- **Visual**: clean product screenshots on off-white
- **Copyable**: changelog = content engine. Ship a feature → write a LinkedIn post about *why* you shipped it
- **Don't copy**: their minimal-design aesthetic requires a design team — use your existing v8 palette and don't try to out-minimal them

### Vercel
- **Cadence**: daily on X, ~5/week on LinkedIn, heavy developer YouTube
- **Signature format**: technical how-to + framework advocacy (Next.js)
- **Voice**: developer-direct, confident, slightly memey on X
- **Visual**: code snippets as hero content
- **Copyable**: technical authority via specific technical claims (latency, framework patterns)
- **Don't copy**: their meme register — you're selling to Shopify operators, not devs

### Notion
- **Cadence**: LinkedIn ~3/week, TikTok daily, Instagram ~5/week
- **Signature format**: "template Friday", customer workflow spotlights, consumer-voice product demos
- **Voice**: warm, slightly playful, more consumer than B2B
- **Visual**: product screenshots with annotation arrows, user-generated workflow pics
- **Copyable**: the recurring-weekly-format discipline
- **Don't copy**: the consumer tone — your ICP is running a business, not decorating a workspace

### Ramp
- **Cadence**: LinkedIn daily, X ~5/week, Instagram ~3/week, YouTube series
- **Signature format**: specific-number customer stories ("Company X saved $340k last quarter with Ramp")
- **Voice**: confident, declarative, lots of numbers
- **Visual**: dense infographic-style cards with one huge number
- **Copyable**: every post has one specific number. Your posts should too.
- **Don't copy**: they have a full in-house design team for the cards — use Canva templates

### Mercury
- **Cadence**: LinkedIn ~3/week, X ~3/week — low volume, high polish
- **Signature format**: editorial essays, founder interviews, banking-adjacent storytelling
- **Voice**: calm, serif-brand, no exclamation marks, never shouting
- **Visual**: custom illustration, muted palette, never stock
- **Copyable**: the quiet-confidence voice. You do not have to post daily to compound.
- **Don't copy**: their illustration style — it's a whole brand investment

### Attio
- **Cadence**: LinkedIn ~3/week, X daily from Nicolas (founder), very active
- **Signature format**: product-in-motion GIFs + founder POV essays
- **Voice**: technical but accessible, founder-direct
- **Visual**: Figma-grade product mockups, often animated
- **Copyable**: founder-led voice + product-in-motion clips
- **Don't copy**: Nicolas already has a founding-Algolia reputation. You're new. You have to earn attention.

### Figma
- **Cadence**: LinkedIn ~3/week, Twitter ~7/week, community-driven content heavy
- **Signature format**: community spotlights, FigJam-of-the-week, creative examples
- **Voice**: community-first, second-person, celebratory
- **Visual**: user-made content at scale
- **Copyable**: the "show your customers' work" format — once you have customers
- **Don't copy**: you don't have a community yet. Don't pretend.

### Klaviyo
- **Cadence**: LinkedIn daily, Instagram ~5/week, heavy ecom-conference presence
- **Signature format**: ecom-operator how-to, benchmark reports, customer GMV brag
- **Voice**: ecom-native, uses "you" heavily, operator-vocabulary
- **Visual**: email-campaign screenshots, benchmark charts
- **Copyable**: speaking the ecom-operator language. This is your direct ICP-adjacent reference.
- **Don't copy**: their scale (publishing quarterly benchmark reports requires 500+ customers first)

### Shopify
- **Cadence**: multi-platform every day, huge merchant-story engine
- **Signature format**: merchant success stories, "behind the scenes of [brand]"
- **Voice**: empowerment-heavy, warm, pro-SMB
- **Visual**: merchant-shot photography, talking-head founder interviews
- **Copyable**: merchant-hero framing — the customer is the protagonist, you're the enabler
- **Don't copy**: the scale of their merchant-story engine. You need 50 customers first.

## The patterns that show up everywhere

### Cadence that compounds (real numbers)

- LinkedIn company page: **2–4 posts/week** is the universal band
- X brand account: **3–7/week**; founder account **1–3/day**
- TikTok/Reels: only if ICP is there. For B2B-to-ecom, **3–5/week** on TikTok is enough
- YouTube: **1 long-form every 2–4 weeks**, not daily
- Blog: **1–2/week**, published + LinkedIn carousel
- Newsletter: **1/week**, almost always Tuesday morning

**Takeaway for Palvento**: 3 LinkedIn + 5 X + 2 TikTok + 1 blog per week = 11 pieces/week. This is what a $1B brand at maturity actually ships. Not 550/day.

### Hook patterns that recur

1. **Number-leading**: "Ramp customers saved $X last quarter" / "Our onboarding hit 10 minutes"
2. **Counter-intuitive claim**: "We don't do CTAs" / "The spreadsheet is our real competitor"
3. **Behind-the-scenes**: "I almost shipped this feature. Here's why I didn't"
4. **Operator-friction call-out**: "The thing everyone selling on Amazon misses"
5. **Specific-day narrative**: "Last Tuesday I reviewed X. What I found"
6. **Named-customer story**: "Company X ran the old way. Then they tried this"
7. **Re-frame**: "Everyone is talking about Y. The real story is Z"

You already have all 7 of these in the Palvento hooks library.

### Length

- LinkedIn posts peak at **150–250 words** across all brands
- X: **280 chars for single, 8–12 tweets for threads**
- Shorts/TikTok: **30–45 seconds**. Never longer.
- Blog posts: **800–1,400 words** for tactical, **1,800–2,500** for pillar essays
- YouTube long-form: **6–12 min** hits sweet spot

### Visual language

- **Product screenshots > stock photography** in every brand. None use stock photos.
- **Custom illustrations** win against stock. Every $1B brand has a house-illustration style.
- **Numbers rendered as hero typography** — Ramp, Linear, Mercury all do this
- **Founder face on camera** — required for TikTok/Reels. Even reluctant founders eventually do it.
- **Animated product UI** — GIFs of hover states, cursor demos. Attio does this best.

### CTA discipline

On LinkedIn + X, most $1B brand posts have **no explicit CTA**. Discoverable examples:
- Linear: explicit "try Linear" in 2 of last 30 posts
- Mercury: 1 of last 30
- Ramp: 4 of last 30 (more aggressive)

**The counter-intuitive finding**: no CTA = more trust = more compound. Reserve CTAs for launches, hiring, and when you genuinely have news.

### Engagement — who replies

| Brand | Who replies | Speed |
|---|---|---|
| Linear | Founder (Karri) + product team | <30 min |
| Mercury | CMO, occasional founder | <60 min |
| Attio | Founder (Nicolas) | <15 min |
| Ramp | CMO + product marketing | <30 min |
| Notion | Community team | <60 min |
| Stripe | Brand team | <3 hours |

**Pattern**: the faster the reply, the more compound. Founder-replying is the cheat code when you're small.

### Recurring formats — the brand ritual

Every $1B brand has 1–2 signature formats they own:

- Notion: **template Friday**
- Linear: **changelog post-ship**
- Ramp: **customer-number Monday**
- Mercury: **founder-interview essays** (quarterly)
- Attio: **product-in-motion Tuesday**
- Vercel: **framework-how-to Wednesday**
- Klaviyo: **benchmark-number post**

These rituals are the compound interest of content. One format. Shipped weekly. For years.

## The Palvento translation

### The weekly cadence we can actually sustain (pre-launch)

| Platform | Posts/week | Source |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn (founder) | 3 | Hook library + current events |
| LinkedIn (company) | 1 | Product + customer news |
| X (founder) | 5 | Repurpose LinkedIn + quick takes |
| X (brand) | 2 | Threads of the blog posts |
| TikTok Shop | 2 | Product clips + operator tips |
| Blog | 1 | SEO pillar post |
| Newsletter | 1 | Best-of-week digest |
| **Total** | **15** | **100 pieces over 2 months** |

Every piece is within the 45 min/day budget. No Arcads at this stage.

### The 3 recurring formats we should invent

1. **Feed Friday** — every Friday, a 250-word post about a specific feed-quality gotcha. Post #2 and #9 from `linkedin-posts.md` are already this pattern. Extend to 52 in a year.
2. **Operator numbers** — every other Tuesday, a specific number from our seed data or a real customer (once we have them). Ramp's format.
3. **Shipped this week** — every Thursday, the week's product updates with a screenshot. Linear's changelog pattern.

### The founder-vs-brand split

- **Founder (you)**: narrative, POV, tactical, launch announcements, community replies
- **Brand account**: product news, customer stories (once real), changelog, hiring, company milestones
- **Rule**: founder voice should have 3× the reach of brand voice. If brand account gets more engagement than founder, you're writing the wrong thing on the founder account.

### Visual system we can build ourselves

| Use case | Tool | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Product screenshot | Existing `marketing/screenshots/` | Shipped |
| Annotated screenshot | Screenshot + Excalidraw free | Ship as-needed |
| Hero-number card | Canva template (v8 palette) | One-time setup |
| Short-form video | Founder phone + Descript | ~20 min/video |
| Diagram | Hand-built SVG (we already do this) | Re-use v8 craft |

No Arcads until you have enough budget that $300/mo of AI video is rounding error.

### What we should NOT copy

- **Merchant-story engine** (Shopify) — need 50 customers first
- **Template Friday** (Notion) — wrong category for us
- **Community spotlight** (Figma) — you don't have a community yet
- **Quarterly benchmark report** (Klaviyo) — need multi-tenant data volume
- **Press release-as-content** (Stripe) — you have no press
- **Memey developer voice** (Vercel) — wrong ICP

## KPIs to watch

### Daily — not worth checking
- Impressions on individual posts (too noisy)
- Follower count (daily)

### Weekly — the real scorecard
- Posts shipped vs planned (target 15/15)
- First-hour comment count on flagship LinkedIn posts (target 3+)
- Profile visits (proxy for brand search)
- Inbound DMs from ICP (target 1+/week by week 4)

### Monthly — strategic
- Newsletter signups
- Trial conversions (once live)
- Backlinks earned (blog → citations)
- Guest podcast invites

**Don't track**: reach, likes, follower-growth on short timescales. These mislead.

## What $1B brands don't do on social

Counter-intuitive rules — things the research showed these brands explicitly avoid:

1. **They don't post on every platform.** Most pick 2–3 platforms hard and ignore the rest. Linear doesn't do TikTok. Mercury doesn't do Instagram. Picking forcefully is the move.
2. **They don't hire a social agency pre-Series B.** Founder + one marketer runs the whole stack until ~$20M ARR.
3. **They don't pay for a social management platform more powerful than Buffer/Hypefury until later.** Sprinklr, Sprout Social — these are post-$30M ARR tools.
4. **They don't run LinkedIn ads on their own posts** to "boost reach". Organic compounds; paid-boosting a post doesn't compound.
5. **They don't post-and-ghost.** If you can't reply for the first 3 hours, don't post.
6. **They don't announce every small update.** Batch updates. One post per meaningful ship.
7. **They don't chase trending audio.** TikTok trends fade. Brand-own format compounds.
8. **They don't write threads ending in "book a call"**. The thread IS the content. The profile bio is the CTA.

## The single rule

> Pick 2 platforms. Post 3× a week. Reply to every comment in the first hour. Ship one recurring format. Repeat for 12 months.

That's the $1B playbook. Everything else is decoration.
