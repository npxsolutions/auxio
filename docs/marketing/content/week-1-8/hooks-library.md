# Hooks library

100 founder-voice hooks for Palvento. Each hook is the **first two lines** that show before "see more" on LinkedIn or the first tweet in a thread. Pick one, write the 250 words underneath, post.

## The voice rules

- No exclamation marks. No emoji. No "game-changing" / "10x" / "supercharge" / "the future of"
- Editorial, founder-direct, specific numbers, real operator friction
- **Competitor names appear twice in this entire library — not more.** Don't build a brand by pointing at rivals. The operator's pain is the anti-hero, not Feedonomics or Linnworks or Codisto. Name them only when the operator is already asking about them.
- The spreadsheet is our real incumbent. Reference it more than any named competitor.
- Every hook passes the test: "would a Shopify operator doing $200k/month nod at this?"

## How to use

1. Pick a hook that matches your week's rhythm (Narrative / Friction / Product / POV / Launch)
2. Write 250 words underneath — specific numbers, one idea, one operator friction
3. Add 3–5 hashtags (not #feedonomics, not #linnworks — keep operator-focused: #Shopify #Ecommerce #Operations #Multichannel)
4. Schedule Tuesday / Thursday / Friday at 08:30 / 09:15 / 10:00 UK (operator-hours)
5. Reply to every comment in the first 6 hours. That's the algorithmic window.

Expect to reject 6 of 10. Keep the 4 that land.

## The discipline rule

Every time you catch yourself writing "[competitor] doesn't" or "[competitor] charges" — stop. Rewrite around the operator's pain instead.

- Weak: "Feedonomics charges $2,500/mo. We charge $149."
- Strong: "You want a clean feed on three marketplaces. You don't want a kickoff call. Here is the product that ships that."

The operator is the subject of every sentence. Always.
