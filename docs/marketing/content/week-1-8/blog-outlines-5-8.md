### Product feed optimization: the feed rules that actually move the needle

**Primary keyword:** "product feed optimization"
**Secondary keywords:** "product feed rules", "feed optimization ecommerce"
**Vol est.:** ~650 · **KD est.:** 24 · **Intent:** Informational/Commercial
**CTA:** Start free trial
**Target WC:** 1,600

**Outline:**

1. **Lead** — Most feed "optimisation" advice is generic: "use good titles", "add keywords", "high-quality images." This post is specific. These are the feed rules that move conversion and prevent suppression, marketplace by marketplace. (~100 words)

2. **What feed optimization actually means** — It is not SEO for marketplaces. It is the discipline of making your product data compliant, discoverable, and conversion-optimised for each channel's specific schema. A title optimised for Shopify is wrong for Amazon. An image that works on eBay fails TikTok Shop's aspect-ratio requirements. (~200 words)

3. **Amazon feed rules that matter** — Six specific rules with before/after examples:
   - Title structure: [Brand] + [Product] + [Key Feature] + [Size/Colour], under 200 chars
   - GTIN validation — submit or request exemption before listing, not after
   - Bullet points: 5 bullets, each under 500 chars, feature-benefit structure
   - Search terms: 250 bytes, no commas, no repetition of title words
   - Browse node accuracy — use the most specific node, not the parent
   - Image requirements: 1000x1000px minimum, pure white background, no text overlays (~400 words)

4. **eBay-specific rules** — Four rules:
   - Item specifics: mandatory fields vary by category, check quarterly
   - Title: 80-char max, front-load the primary keyword
   - Condition descriptions: required for refurbished/used
   - Catalogue matching: submit UPC/EAN to match eBay's catalogue entry (~200 words)

5. **TikTok Shop-specific rules** — Three rules:
   - Image aspect ratio: 1:1 required, lifestyle images encouraged
   - Title: 34-char display limit on mobile, front-load the hook
   - Category compliance: restricted categories require pre-approval (~200 words)

6. **How Palvento automates this** — The feed rules engine validates against each marketplace's schema before submission. Errors surface in the error hub with clear remediation. No manual checking across five dashboards. (~200 words)

7. **CTA** — "Stop fixing feed errors after suppression. Catch them before submission. Try Palvento free." (~50 words)
