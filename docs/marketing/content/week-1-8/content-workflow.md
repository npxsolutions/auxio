# Meridia content workflow — one page

Post 3× per week on LinkedIn, 5× on X, 3× on TikTok/Reels, 1× blog post per week. Build the founder brand. Compound for 52 weeks. Don't miss a week.

## Daily — 45 minutes

| Time | Action | Tool |
|---|---|---|
| 10 min | Reply to comments / DMs from yesterday's post | LinkedIn + X native |
| 20 min | Draft tomorrow's post (pick from `hooks-library.md`, write 250 words) | Claude + Notion |
| 10 min | Clip a 30-sec line from the draft → Arcads video script | Arcads |
| 5 min | Schedule draft into Buffer / Hypefury | Buffer |

## Weekly — 2 hours, Sunday evening

| Task | Time |
|---|---|
| Write 1 blog post (~1000 words) | 60 min |
| Record 1 Loom or short-form video | 15 min |
| Look at last week's analytics (3 wins, 3 misses) | 15 min |
| Schedule the next 3 LinkedIn posts + 5 X posts | 20 min |
| Plan hooks for next week | 10 min |

## Monthly — 4 hours, last Sunday of the month

| Task |
|---|
| Record a guest podcast appearance (pre-booked) |
| Ship 1 customer case study (when you have customers) |
| Run 1 paid-ad experiment with 4 Arcads variants, $200 budget |
| Audit the content calendar — what's stale, what to deprecate |

## What to automate vs keep manual

| Automate | Keep manual |
|---|---|
| Scheduling posts (Buffer/Hypefury) | Replying to comments |
| Drafting variations (Claude) | Final edit + approval |
| Repurposing long→short (Opus/Descript) | Founder voice in POV posts |
| Arcads video generation from a script | DM conversations |
| Blog SEO optimization (Surfer/Frase) | Answering questions in replies |

**Never automate comments or DMs.** LinkedIn will shadow-ban. X will flag.

## The commitments that matter

1. **Don't miss a week.** Consistency beats quality. Fifty-two weeks of mediocre posts outperforms twenty weeks of excellent posts with gaps.
2. **Reply to every comment in the first 6 hours.** This is when the algorithm watches for engagement signal. Miss this and the post dies.
3. **Write 250 words or less.** Every hook earns at most 250 words of body. Brevity compounds.
4. **One idea per post.** If you're adding a second, it's a second post.
5. **Specific numbers.** "1,340 dollars in fees" beats "a lot of fees". Always.
6. **Real operator friction.** Every post has to pass: "would an operator I know nod at this?"
7. **No exclamation marks.** No emoji. No "game-changing" / "10x" / "the future of". Editorial voice.

## What success looks like at 12 weeks

- **1,500 LinkedIn followers** from ~500 today — 100/week is the target
- **2,000 X followers** from current baseline — threads drive this faster than LinkedIn
- **500 monthly newsletter subscribers** — gated by the first blog post with a CTA
- **3–5 inbound demo requests per week** — the content is working
- **1 guest podcast every 4 weeks** — you pitched yourself
- **5 customer case studies in the backlog** — 1 case study per 2 paid customers

If week 12 doesn't hit 2 of these 6, change one thing. Not all six. One.

## What success looks like at 52 weeks

- **10k+ LinkedIn followers** — industry-known founder in the Shopify-multichannel space
- **20k+ X followers** — compound from consistent threads
- **5k newsletter** — owned channel that doesn't die when algorithms shift
- **50+ case studies**
- **1 weekly podcast guest booking** as standard
- **Conference speaker invite** — ShopTalk, NRF, eCommerce Summit
- **Referenced by name** in industry category pieces (analyst reports, Shopify blog, BigCommerce blog, operator podcasts)

That's when the content engine is a flywheel. Not in month 3. In month 12.

## The habit stack that makes this work

1. Every morning, 8am, 20 min of writing — before email
2. Every Sunday, 2 hours blocked for the week ahead — non-negotiable
3. Last Sunday of the month, 4 hours blocked for the month ahead
4. Phone on Do Not Disturb during writing blocks
5. Publishing happens in Buffer — you don't need to open LinkedIn to post
6. Comment replies happen on the phone, not the desktop
7. Track one number weekly: post count shipped ÷ post count planned. If that drops below 0.8, reset.

The whole thing is 45 minutes a day. Do it 300 times and you have a business.
