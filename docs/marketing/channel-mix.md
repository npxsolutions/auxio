# Channel Mix

GTM channels ranked by expected leverage for Auxio at current stage (pre-PMF expansion, post-launch v8, <100 paying customers). Ranking is *leverage*, not *volume* — a channel that produces 5 ideal-fit Tier 2 customers beats a channel that produces 50 Tier 1 trials.

Ownership notes assume a team of 2 (founder + part-time content lead) until Day 90.

---

## 1. SEO / content — **highest leverage, slowest to compound**
- **CAC estimate.** $40–$120 blended once ranking, effectively $0 at the margin once the post exists.
- **Time to first customer.** 8–14 weeks. First ranked post around week 6, first trial from organic around week 10.
- **What works.** Category-defining essays ("What is a Commerce Operations Platform?"), head-to-head `/vs/*` pages (we have four, need eight), tactical guides with real numbers (pricing-floors, P&L worked examples).
- **What doesn't.** Generic listicles ("10 Best Inventory Tools"). AI-generated filler. Keyword-stuffed content that reads like 2018.
- **Weekly cadence.** 2 posts/week (1 pillar + 1 supporting). See `content/seo-calendar.md`.
- **Owner.** Founder writes the opinionated pieces. Contract writer (Upwork, ~$400/post) drafts supporting posts, founder edits.

## 2. Partnerships (agencies, 3PLs, app-store co-marketing) — **second highest leverage**
- **CAC estimate.** $200–$400 (mostly rev-share, not cash).
- **Time to first customer.** 4–8 weeks per partner.
- **What works.** Agencies that already serve Tier 2 operators (Eastside Co, Underwaterpistol, Swanky, Blend, Glow). 3PLs looking for value-add (ShipBob, ShipHero's partner ecosystem). Warm intros — no cold partner outreach.
- **What doesn't.** Directory listings with no relationship. Reseller agreements without shared GTM commitment.
- **Weekly cadence.** 2 partner conversations/week, 1 co-marketing asset/month (joint webinar, guest post, listing).
- **Owner.** Founder. Non-delegable for first 20 partners.

## 3. Marketplace listings (Shopify App Store, BigCommerce, TikTok Shop) — **high leverage, slow approval**
- **CAC estimate.** $0 at the margin, but 3–6 weeks of build + review time per marketplace.
- **Time to first customer.** 10–14 weeks (approval + rank).
- **What works.** Shopify App Store is the single biggest channel for Tier 2 self-serve. Video demo, 5-star seeding from existing customers, weekly iteration on listing copy.
- **What doesn't.** Listing once and forgetting. Shopify's algorithm rewards recency and review velocity.
- **Weekly cadence.** One listing asset update/week (screenshot, changelog, review request).
- **Owner.** Founder + designer.

## 4. Cold outreach — **high leverage early, diminishing returns fast**
- **CAC estimate.** $150–$300 at 1% reply-to-close.
- **Time to first customer.** 2–4 weeks.
- **What works.** Hand-picked lists of Shopify stores doing >$100k/yr on Amazon + Shopify (scraped via Seller Directory + Ahrefs). Editorial, plain-text email. Specific hook per recipient ("Saw you launched on TikTok Shop last month — here's how Auxio would slot in"). Five-touch sequence (see `outreach/cold-email-sequence.md`).
- **What doesn't.** Generic "I'd love to chat" emails. Automation without personalization. Buying Apollo lists.
- **Weekly cadence.** 100 hand-picked prospects/week, 5-touch sequence, expect 3–5 replies.
- **Owner.** Founder for first 100 prospects, then contracted SDR.

## 5. Communities — **moderate leverage, reputation compounding**
- **CAC estimate.** $0 cash, 2–3 hours/week.
- **Time to first customer.** 3–6 weeks.
- **What works.** Helping in *Ecommerce Fuel* forum, *Operators* Slack, r/ecommerce, r/FulfillmentByAmazon. Answering questions with real expertise, mentioning Auxio only when directly relevant. Indie Hackers for build-in-public narrative (Tier 1 reach).
- **What doesn't.** Drive-by self-promotion. "We just launched!" posts in every Slack. Reddit spam will get the account banned and the brand blacklisted.
- **Weekly cadence.** 3 meaningful posts/replies per week, across 3 communities max.
- **Owner.** Founder. Impossible to delegate without losing authenticity.

## 6. Product Hunt + Hacker News launch — **one-time spike, long tail via SEO**
- **CAC estimate.** $0 cash, ~40 hours of prep.
- **Time to first customer.** Day of launch.
- **What works.** Coordinated launch (PH + HN + LinkedIn + email list + community pings within a 6-hour window). Real first-comment from founder with a demo link. A hunter with an engaged following. See `launch/product-hunt-kit.md` and `launch/hn-post.md`.
- **What doesn't.** Upvote-buying, alarmist landing pages, "#1 on Product Hunt!" humblebrags after the fact.
- **Weekly cadence.** N/A — single launch week in 90-day plan.
- **Owner.** Founder.

## 7. Paid ads (Google Search, Meta) — **deferred until PMF signal is clearer**
- **CAC estimate.** $250–$600 on Google Search at current SERP competitiveness. Meta higher and lower intent.
- **Time to first customer.** 3–7 days from campaign live.
- **What works.** Branded + competitor-term Google Search ("Linnworks alternative", "Brightpearl pricing", "multichannel inventory software"). Tight match types. Direct-to-demo or direct-to-trial landing pages. Do not run Meta until there's a working Tier 1 self-serve funnel — the intent mismatch burns money.
- **What doesn't.** Generic "ecommerce software" terms ($40+ CPC, low intent). YouTube pre-roll. LinkedIn ads (retargeting only).
- **Weekly cadence.** Once started: daily budget capped, weekly creative review.
- **Owner.** Contracted performance marketer (~$1.5k/mo retainer once spend > $5k/mo).

## 8. Influencer (commerce YouTubers, newsletter authors) — **moderate leverage, hard to scale**
- **CAC estimate.** $300–$800 per sponsored placement.
- **Time to first customer.** 1–3 weeks from ship.
- **What works.** *My Amazon Guy*, *Ecommerce Fuel* newsletter, *2PM* (paid sponsor slot), Nathan Resnick's newsletter. One-off placements to test, then renew the ones that convert.
- **What doesn't.** TikTok creators with large followings but no operator audience. Influencer agencies taking 30%.
- **Weekly cadence.** 1 placement/month.
- **Owner.** Founder sources, part-time content lead manages logistics.

## 9. Affiliate program — **passive leverage after ~Day 60**
- **CAC estimate.** 20% first-year rev-share, ~$60–$300 effective per customer.
- **Time to first customer.** 4–8 weeks after launch of `/affiliates` push.
- **What works.** Targeted outreach to newsletter authors (Polly, *The Hustle*, *2PM*, *Morning Brew Retail*) and agency operators. Unique link, recurring commission, monthly payout.
- **What doesn't.** Public "anyone can sign up" affiliate programs. Coupon sites. Signal-chasing.
- **Weekly cadence.** 1 affiliate recruited/week from Day 30 onwards.
- **Owner.** Part-time content lead, founder approves.

## 10. Conferences (NRF, eTail, Shoptalk) — **Scale Operator channel, expensive**
- **CAC estimate.** $3k–$8k per closed-won Scale Operator.
- **Time to first customer.** 8–16 weeks post-conference (long cycles).
- **What works.** Small booths or no-booth lobby presence at eTail and Retail Global. Private dinners (8–12 operators) > booth traffic. Focus on the top 5 Scale prospects, not the 500 swipe-badges.
- **What doesn't.** Big booths, swag, sponsoring keynotes before you have 20+ reference customers.
- **Weekly cadence.** N/A — one event/quarter max in Year 1.
- **Owner.** Founder.

## 11. Webinars — **moderate leverage, repeatable**
- **CAC estimate.** $80–$200 per attendee, 5–10% trial conversion.
- **Time to first customer.** Week of webinar.
- **What works.** Tactical, no-slideware, 45 minutes, co-hosted with a partner agency or a recognizable operator. First one outlined in `launch/webinar-1-outline.md`.
- **What doesn't.** Product pitch disguised as education. Hour-plus runtime. Registration walls with 12 form fields.
- **Weekly cadence.** One webinar every 3 weeks.
- **Owner.** Founder hosts, part-time content lead produces.

## Explicitly not pursuing in first 90 days

TV, podcast ads (until there's a working paid funnel), TikTok creator marketing, SEO agencies, LinkedIn ads, outbound-as-a-service firms, Capterra/G2 reviews farming, "AI-generated SEO content at scale".
