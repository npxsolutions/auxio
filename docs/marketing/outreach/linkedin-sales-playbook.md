# LinkedIn Selling Playbook — Palvento Founder Outbound

> Everything needed to sell on LinkedIn as a solo-founder SaaS. Narrow wedge: Shopify merchants running Google Shopping but not on eBay, $100k–$500k/mo GMV, Tier 2 in `icp.md`. Pairs with `google-ebay-wedge-playbook.md`.

---

## Before you send anything — profile prerequisites

A cold DM that points at a profile saying "CEO, NPX Solutions" when the pitch is for Palvento gets ignored. Fix these five fields before the first connection request goes out:

1. **Headline** (120 chars) — the part under your name. Optimize for prospect scanning, not for "LinkedIn best practices":
   > `Building Palvento — Shopify → eBay & Google Shopping in 10 minutes · ex-[your prior credibility]`

2. **About section** (2000 char limit, first 300 visible) — lead with the problem and who you solve it for. Three paragraphs:
   - One-liner on who Palvento is for (Shopify merchants launching a second channel)
   - What you built and why it's different (self-serve, $149, no enterprise call)
   - What you're looking for (founding customers, honest feedback, intros to ops-heavy Shopify stores)

3. **Featured section** — pin three items **in this order**:
   - A 30-second Loom of the OAuth-to-first-push flow
   - The `/pricing` page
   - Your best LinkedIn post of the week (auto-featured; rotate weekly)

4. **Experience → current role** — edit to say `Founder, Palvento — Shopify multichannel feed management`. Point to `palvento.com`. Add a sentence saying what the tool does in plain English.

5. **Banner image** — a simple product screenshot with the headline "Shopify → eBay + Google Shopping." Not a generic gradient; prospects who click through should see the product immediately. (Use Canva when you've got the MCP tools surfaced — should take 5 min.)

If any of those five is still pre-Palvento when you send your first connection, pause and fix them. The profile carries 40% of the reply rate.

## Prospecting — Sales Nav filters

Same filters as the Google-eBay playbook, restated for quick reference:

| Filter                    | Value                                                                    |
|---------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Company headcount         | `2–10` primary, `11–50` secondary                                        |
| Industry                  | `Retail`, `Apparel & Fashion`, `Consumer Goods`, `Cosmetics`, `Food & Beverage` |
| Company keywords          | `Shopify`, `DTC`, `direct to consumer`, `ecommerce brand`                |
| Seniority                 | `Owner`, `Founder`, `CXO`, `VP`, `Director`                              |
| Job title                 | `Founder` OR `Ecommerce Manager` OR `Head of Operations` OR `Ecommerce Director` |
| Geography                 | `UK` + `US` first; `Canada`, `Australia` second                          |
| Exclude keywords          | `Amazon.com`, `Walmart`, `eBay` (company names false-positive)           |
| TeamLink                  | On — prioritise prospects with shared connections (3× acceptance rate)   |

**Save as a Sales Nav lead list** named `palvento-t2-wedge-cohort1`. Cap the cohort at **60 prospects** — you'll qualify down to ~20 that fit the Google-Shopping-but-not-on-eBay wedge.

### Qualification order (60 → 20)

For each prospect, in this order:
1. **Warm signal** — shared connection? If yes, keep, tag `warm`.
2. **Shopify verified** — BuiltWith Chrome extension on their company site. Drop if not Shopify.
3. **Running Google Shopping** — Google their top product SKU. See Shopping ad? Keep.
4. **Not on eBay** — search eBay for brand. <10 listings = keep, tag `eBay-empty`. >10 = drop (different pitch).

Expected survival rate ~30%. 60 → ~18–22 qualified.

## Connection requests — 3 variants, 200-char hard cap

LinkedIn now caps request notes at **200 characters** on free accounts (300 on Premium). Every character counts. Write without spammy words ("revolutionize", "leverage", "synergy") — LinkedIn's algorithm scores those down.

### Variant 1 — Mutual-connection warm

> Hi {{first}}, saw we both know {{mutual}}. Building Palvento — self-serve multichannel for Shopify. Would love to compare notes on how {{store}} is thinking about eBay.

**Use when:** TeamLink shows a shared connection. Acceptance rate ~55–65%.

### Variant 2 — Content engagement

> Hi {{first}}, commented on your post about {{topic}} last week — genuine question about how {{store}} splits GMV between Google Shopping and marketplaces. Would be good to connect.

**Use when:** you've already engaged on their post (you *must* actually have commented — this is not a lie vector). Acceptance rate ~45–55%.

### Variant 3 — Cold, problem-led

> Hi {{first}}, noticed {{store}} runs Google Shopping but isn't on eBay yet. Founder of Palvento — we ship that in 10 min. Not pitching here, just keen to connect.

**Use when:** no warm signal, haven't engaged their content. Acceptance rate ~15–25% — still the dominant volume channel. The "not pitching here" is honest: you won't pitch in the request, you'll pitch in the first DM.

## First DM after connection accepted — curiosity, not pitch

The common failure: connection accepts, founder DMs a 3-paragraph pitch within 60 seconds, prospect ghosts. The better pattern: ask a genuine qualifying question that earns a reply, *then* pitch on the strength of their answer.

### Day 0, ~2 hours after accept

> Thanks for connecting, {{first}}. Genuine question before I pitch anything: is eBay on the roadmap for {{store}} in the next 90 days, or is Google Shopping the full plan for now?
>
> Not trying to sell you something you don't want — just calibrating where Palvento fits.

Three possible replies, three next moves:

| Reply                                         | Next DM                                                                                                                                          |
|-----------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| "Yes, we're planning eBay"                    | Drop the pitch: "Here's a 30-sec Loom showing the Shopify → eBay flow. Free to trial for 14 days, $149/mo after." (+ Loom link + `palvento.com`)  |
| "We tried eBay, it didn't work"               | Ask why. Most cite listing quality / inventory drift — that's exactly what we solve. "If I rebuild the feed against your Shopify catalog as a demo, would you look?" |
| "Google Shopping is enough for us"            | Don't force it. "Understood. If the plan ever shifts, you know where to find me. In the meantime, happy to pass on an operator I rate if you're hiring." — build optionality, long-game them. |

### Day 4, if no reply to Day 0

> Quick bump {{first}} — the Loom version of the question, in case reading 3 paragraphs felt like homework: *[30-sec Loom link]*. 15 sec of your time.

### Day 10, final

> Last note — I'll close the thread so your DMs stay clean. If Shopify-to-eBay ever comes back on the table, `palvento.com`, self-serve trial, no call required. Wishing {{store}} a strong quarter.

## Do NOT do on LinkedIn

- **Never** auto-connect/auto-DM via Dripify, Expandi, LinkedHelper, or any other automation tool. LinkedIn's account-risk model has caught up; restrictions hit within 2–4 weeks and reputation loss on a founder profile is permanent.
- **Don't** pitch in the connection request. The 200 chars are for earning the acceptance, not closing the sale.
- **Don't** send a voice note in the first DM. Only after a reply.
- **Don't** tag prospects in posts to pull them into comments. Reads as desperate.
- **Don't** send "just checking in" bumps without new information. Every bump must add something: a Loom, a customer anecdote, a data point.
- **Don't** DM on Saturday / Sunday. Reply rate halves.
- **Don't** use InMail credits on prospects you haven't manually qualified. Credit burn rate of >10 InMails per meeting booked means the filters are wrong.

## Rate limits — LinkedIn will restrict you if you cross these

| Action                                | Safe daily cap (free)   | Safe daily cap (Premium + Sales Nav) |
|---------------------------------------|--------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| Connection requests                   | 20/day                  | 30/day (LinkedIn *policy* cap: 100/week) |
| DMs to connections                    | 50/day                  | 100/day                               |
| InMails                               | n/a                     | Cap by plan — don't burn on cold      |
| Profile views                         | 150/day                 | 300/day                               |
| Post likes / comments                 | No practical cap        | No practical cap                      |

Ramp: **10 connections/day week 1, 15 week 2, 20 week 3+.** Don't step-function to 30 on day 1 — that's the signal LinkedIn's anti-abuse model flags.

## Success signals for cohort 1 (60 LinkedIn prospects)

| Metric                                   | Target      | Diagnostic if missed                                                    |
|------------------------------------------|-------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Connection acceptance rate               | ≥ 35%       | Profile not aligned; request copy generic; list too broad               |
| Reply rate (Day 0 DM)                    | ≥ 25%       | Opener pitches instead of asks; first line feels templated              |
| "Yes, interested in demo"                | ≥ 15% of replies | Pitch doesn't land on the problem they have                          |
| Trials started                           | ≥ 50% of demos booked | Friction between call and signup; OAuth failing; onboarding poor |
| Paying customers                         | ≥ 3 out of 60 prospects | End-to-end funnel broken somewhere above                            |

If acceptance < 25% after 20 sends, stop sending and fix the profile first. The list isn't the problem.
