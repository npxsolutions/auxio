# Palvento — Investor One-Pager

**Per-channel P&L for multichannel commerce. The financial OS for the $4T cross-border seller economy.**

---

## The problem

Multichannel sellers can't tell you which channel actually makes them money.

A merchant on Shopify, eBay, Amazon, Etsy, and TikTok Shop sees five marketplaces' revenue dashboards, five fee schedules, five payout cadences, five FX policies, and zero unified contribution-margin truth. Every existing tool — Linnworks, ChannelAdvisor, Brightpearl, Sellbrite, Channable — ships listings and orders. None reconcile fees, returns, ad spend, and FX to the SKU. Sellers run multichannel ops on spreadsheets and instinct, then wonder why their fastest-growing channel destroys margin.

The financial blind spot is the #1 unsolved CFO problem in commerce.

## Why now

Three forcing functions converging in 2026:

1. **Fees up, margins down.** Amazon FBA fees rose 30% YoY in 2025. eBay introduced new "Promoted Listings Standard" rates. Shopify Markets added cross-border processing fees. Sellers can no longer assume sale = profit.
2. **Cross-border default.** TikTok Shop launched globally; Shopify Markets made every store multi-currency by default. Single-region operators are being forced multichannel even when they don't want to be.
3. **AI-native operations is finally credible.** LLMs can read fee schedules, parse settlement reports, and reconcile transactions at human-grade accuracy — a job that previously needed a £40k/year ops analyst.

The seller market that incumbents serve (mid-market UK/EU multichannel) is now feeling acute fee pressure, regulatory complexity, and AI-driven margin compression *all at once*. The window for a new entrant with a fundamentally different value proposition is open and closing within 24 months.

## The wedge

Palvento is the per-channel P&L platform for multichannel sellers — the only operations layer that reconciles fees, returns, fulfilment, and ad spend to the SKU level across every marketplace, currency, and region.

We are not a feed manager. We are not a listing tool. We are not a repricer. Those are the *means*. The *product* is the truth: which channel, which SKU, this month, makes you money. Linnworks tells you what sold; Palvento tells you what made you money.

Self-serve install in 10 minutes — no enterprise sales call, no 30-day onboarding. Five currencies native. Multi-region by default.

## Expansion path: the multi-billion thesis

The wedge gets us to $20M ARR. The expansion gets us past $1B.

Once Palvento is the merchant's source of fee truth, we own the data shape that unlocks:

1. **Working capital** — every transaction's true margin is visible to us. We can underwrite same-day capital advances against future channel payouts. (Wayflyer: $1.6B; Settle: $500M+)
2. **FX optimisation** — we see every multi-currency settlement. We can route payouts through best-rate corridors and capture the spread. (Airwallex: $5.6B)
3. **Fast payouts** — marketplaces hold funds for 7-14 days; we have receivables visibility. We can advance payouts at a fee. (Karat: $300M+; Pipe: $2B at peak)
4. **Embedded insurance** — return-rate and dispute-rate data lets us underwrite multichannel-specific cover. (Coalition's model, applied vertical.)
5. **Tax compliance & reporting** — multichannel multi-currency tax is a nightmare; we already have the data. (Avalara: $9B public.)

This is the Klaviyo arc — own a single high-value insight (email revenue attribution → fee truth), then expand into the financial layer of the same buyer's stack.
