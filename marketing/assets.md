## 7. Objection Handling

### "It's too expensive."
**Response:** Compare it to what you're spending now. If manual management costs you 5 hours a week at any reasonable rate, you're already spending more than Fulcra costs. And we start at £79/month — not £1,000 like Feedonomics. There's a 7-day free trial. The risk is zero.

### "I already use [Linnworks / Veeqo / another tool]."
**Response:** Those are order management tools. Fulcra is feed management — it's about getting your product data formatted correctly and published to each channel's requirements. They solve different problems. Many sellers use both. If you're on Linnworks and still manually uploading listings to eBay and Amazon, Fulcra fills that gap.

### "I'm worried it'll be complicated to set up."
**Response:** Connect takes 2 minutes via OAuth — same as logging into any app. Import is a CSV upload or a Shopify sync. Publishing is a button. If you can manage a Shopify store, you can use Fulcra. There's no developer, no onboarding call, no configuration file.

### "What if I need to switch back?"
**Response:** Your listings stay live on every channel when you cancel. Fulcra doesn't own your product data — you do. Cancel and everything stays exactly as it was. Month-to-month means no lock-in risk.

### "I don't have enough SKUs to justify it."
**Response:** The break-even is lower than you think. If you have 20 products on Shopify and you want them on eBay — that's 20 listings you'd otherwise create manually, formatted for eBay's requirements. Fulcra does that in one import. One channel expansion justifies the cost.

### "I don't trust a new tool with my live listings."
**Response:** Fulcra doesn't touch your existing listings. It creates new ones. You test it with one or two products, see them go live correctly, then scale. There's no risk to what's already running.

## 8. Tone & Style Rules

- Write like a seller explaining to another seller — direct, no fluff
- Never say "seamlessly", "powerful", "robust", "game-changing", "revolutionary"
- Lead with the outcome, follow with the mechanic
- Assume the reader is skeptical. Don't oversell — prove it
- Use real numbers wherever possible (£79, 2 minutes, 3 channels)
- Short sentences. One idea per sentence.
- If you can say it in 10 words, don't use 20
- Competitor comparisons are allowed — be specific, not vague ("Feedonomics" not "enterprise tools")
