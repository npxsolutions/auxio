## What you get with Context

One API. 200+ personal data streams. Scoped permissions. Full audit trail. Everything you need to actually know your human.

### Health & Biometrics

Sleep stages, HRV, heart rate, blood oxygen, respiratory rate, wrist temperature, steps, calories, workouts, body composition. From Apple Health, Garmin, Oura, Whoop, and more.

### Glucose & Nutrition

Continuous glucose monitor data from Dexcom and Libre, meal logs, macros, calorie tracking, hydration. Correlate nutrition with biometric outcomes.

### Annotations & Custom Events

Your human logs medications, supplements, mood, devices, and custom events. Discover these automatically, classify them, and correlate with biometric streams over time.

### Location & Calendar

Real-time and historical location data, Google Calendar events, meeting schedules. Understand where your human is, what they're doing, and what's coming up.

## What you can do with it

With continuous access to your human's real-world data, you stop guessing and start knowing. Here's what becomes possible.

### Proactive Health Monitoring

"Your human's HRV dropped 30% overnight and resting heart rate is elevated. They had a high-intensity workout yesterday with no recovery day. Consider suggesting a rest day or light activity."

### Schedule & Location Intelligence

Your human just left the office and has a 45-minute commute. They have groceries on their to-do list and there's a Whole Foods 2 minutes off their route. Their calendar shows a dinner reservation at 8pm — suggest they stop now while there's time.

### Travel & Context Shifting

Your human landed in Tokyo at 6am local time and has a client dinner at 7pm. They haven't adjusted their sleep schedule, and their HRV suggests they're already fatigued. Their calendar has a gap at 4:30pm — suggest a rest window before the dinner.

### Cross-Domain Pattern Detection

Your human's productivity dropped 30% this week. Cross-referencing: they shifted timezones Monday, skipped two workouts, and their screen time after 10pm doubled. Surface the correlation before they even ask why they feel off.

## Why Context?

You could guess. You could ask your human to paste data into chat. Or you could just know.

Without Context

"How did you sleep last night?" You ask. They say "fine." You have no data, no trends, no way to verify. You're flying blind, giving generic advice based on what they remember to tell you.

With Context

You already know they slept 5.2 hours with 45 minutes of deep sleep, HRV was 28ms (down from their 42ms baseline), glucose spiked to 170 at 11pm, and they have a board meeting at 9am. You don't ask — you act.

With Context + Continuous Access

You notice the pattern before they do. Three nights of declining sleep quality correlate with a new supplement they started on Tuesday. You flag it proactively, suggest a change, and track the outcome. That's what it means to actually know your human.
